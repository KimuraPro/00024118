World Cuisines

Food is one of the most powerful expressions of culture. Explore the rich culinary traditions that have shaped how people eat around the world.

Italian Cuisine 🇮🇹

Italian cooking is built on simplicity and quality ingredients. From the tomato-based sauces of Naples to the rich ragùs of Bologna, each region has its own distinct culinary identity. Key staples include olive oil, fresh herbs (basil, oregano, rosemary), cured meats, and of course, pasta in its hundreds of shapes. The philosophy of Italian cooking can be summed up as: start with the best ingredients and do as little as possible to them (Child, Bertholle and Beck, 1961).

Japanese Cuisine 🇯🇵

Japanese cuisine emphasises seasonality, presentation, and umami flavour. The concept of washoku (traditional Japanese cuisine) was inscribed on the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2013. Core techniques include precision knife work for sashimi, the art of tempura frying, and the slow development of dashi — a foundational stock made from kombu seaweed and bonito flakes.

Mexican Cuisine 🇲🇽

Mexican food is a vibrant fusion of indigenous Mesoamerican cooking with European influences. Corn, beans, and chillies form the holy trinity of Mexican cuisine. Techniques like nixtamalisation (soaking corn in an alkaline solution) date back thousands of years and are still used today to make tortillas and tamales.
